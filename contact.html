About Ben Athem

Ben Athem writes quiet, character-driven speculative fiction about people learning to become home for one another.

Whether set aboard a failing colony station, an ancient alien vessel, or somewhere touched by magic, his stories are less interested in extraordinary worlds than in the ordinary acts of kindness that allow people to survive them.

His work explores belonging, loneliness, hope, curiosity and compassion, often through unlikely relationships between people who begin by misunderstanding one another and end by choosing, day after day, to remain.

Rather than relying on destiny or dramatic declarations, his romances grow from countless small moments: a shared silence, an offered hand, and the decision to notice someone else’s needs before they are spoken.

His science fiction asks not, “What will the future look like?” but, “What kind of people do we hope to become?”

His fantasy asks not, “What if magic existed?” but, “Would love become any less ordinary if it did?”

At the heart of every story is the belief that kindness is never weakness, that understanding is always worth pursuing, and that home is not always somewhere you return to.

Sometimes...

it is someone who quietly chooses to stay.
